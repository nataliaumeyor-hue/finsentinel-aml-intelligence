# FINSENTINEL™ AI Use Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Pre-commercial development  
**Last Updated:** 2026  
**Owner:** Natalia Umeyor, Founder, FINSENTINEL™  
**Aligned With:** NIST AI RMF 1.0 — GOVERN Function

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the development, deployment, and use of artificial
intelligence and machine learning systems within the FINSENTINEL™ platform.
It establishes the principles, responsibilities, and controls that ensure
FINSENTINEL™ operates as a trustworthy, accountable, and responsible AI
system in accordance with U.S. federal standards.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All AI and ML models developed or deployed within the FINSENTINEL™ platform
- All personnel, contractors, and advisors involved in model development,
  testing, or deployment
- All third-party data providers and technology partners
- All client institutions and government agencies accessing FINSENTINEL™
  intelligence outputs

---

## 3. Core Principles

| Principle | Commitment |
|---|---|
| **Human Oversight** | No automated enforcement action — all SAR filings and compliance decisions require human review and approval before execution |
| **Transparency** | Model architecture, training data documentation, performance metrics, and limitations are publicly disclosed in this repository |
| **Fairness** | Regular bias and fairness testing across entity types, geographies, transaction structures, and demographic proxies |
| **Privacy** | Data collection limited to what is strictly necessary for financial crime detection; no unnecessary PII collection or retention |
| **Accountability** | Clear chain of responsibility from model output to human decision-maker at every stage of the intelligence workflow |
| **Security** | Government-grade security controls applied to all data at rest and in transit; FedRAMP Moderate alignment |

---

## 4. Permitted Uses

FINSENTINEL™ AI systems are permitted for use in the following contexts:

- Financial crime signal detection and triage
- Suspicious activity prioritisation for human compliance officer review
- AML alert generation and SAR filing preparation (subject to human approval)
- Entity relationship mapping and beneficial ownership inference
- Regulatory intelligence reporting to FinCEN, OFAC, FBI, and DHS
- Internal compliance monitoring and audit support

---

## 5. Prohibited Uses

FINSENTINEL™ AI systems must not be used for:

- **Credit decisioning** — determining creditworthiness or loan eligibility
- **Employment screening** — hiring, firing, or promotion decisions
- **Law enforcement prosecution** — use as sole or primary evidence in
  criminal proceedings without independent verification
- **Surveillance beyond financial crime scope** — monitoring of individuals
  not suspected of financial crime activity
- **Automated enforcement** — any action taken without human review and
  approval of AI-generated outputs
- **Discriminatory profiling** — targeting individuals based on protected
  characteristics rather than financial crime indicators

## 7. Model Lifecycle Governance

### Development
- All new models must be documented with a system card prior to deployment
- Training data must be documented including source, volume, and governance
- Bias and fairness testing must be completed before any production deployment

### Deployment
- All model deployments logged with version number, date, and change summary
- Performance baseline established at deployment for ongoing monitoring

### Monitoring
- Weekly automated benchmarking against holdout validation set
- Model drift detection pipeline monitors for performance degradation
- Alert threshold recalibration on 30-day cycles

### Retirement
- F1 Score sustained below 0.82 triggers mandatory human review
- F1 Score sustained below 0.75 triggers automatic model suspension
- All retired models retained in version control for audit purposes

---

## 8. Data Governance

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Data minimisation | Collect only data necessary for financial crime detection |
| PII handling | No unnecessary PII collection; all PII encrypted AES-256 at rest |
| Data residency | U.S.-only for all PII and regulated financial data |
| Retention | Raw feeds: 90 days; processed signals: 7 years (BSA requirement) |
| Third-party data | Vendor risk assessment required; contractual data quality SLAs mandatory |
| Dark web data | Non-PII marketplace and forum content only; legal review completed |

---

## 9. Incident Response

In the event of an AI system incident:

1. **Detect** — Automated monitoring or human report identifies incident
2. **Contain** — Affected model suspended or rolled back within 4 hours
3. **Assess** — Root cause analysis completed within 48 hours
4. **Remediate** — Model patched or retrained; controls updated
5. **Notify** — Affected clients and regulatory contacts notified per SLA
6. **Document** — Full incident report added to version control
